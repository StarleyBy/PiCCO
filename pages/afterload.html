Afterload Assessment: Systemic Vascular Resistance Index SVRI

The Systemic Vascular Resistance Index SVRI is the primary hemodynamic parameter used to quantitatively assess afterload - the cumulative resistance the heart (particularly the left ventricle) must overcome to eject blood into the systemic circulation.

Definition and Calculation

Definition

Systemic Vascular Resistance SVR is the calculated resistance of all peripheral blood vessels in the body.

Calculation

SVRI is calculated using an analogy of Ohm's law for circulation. It represents the pressure gradient across the entire systemic circulation divided by flow CI:

SVRI = ((MAP - CVP) / CI) √ó Conversion Constant

  • MAP (Mean Arterial Pressure): Pressure driving flow
  • CVP (Central Venous Pressure): Pressure returning flow (approximately right atrial pressure)
  • CI (Cardiac Index): Flow through the circuit

Since MAP and CI are continuously measured by the PiCCO system, SVRI is a continuously available and important parameter.

Clinical Significance: Vasopressor Therapy Guidance

SVRI is important for differentiating shock states and guiding the use of vasopressors (to increase SVRI) and vasodilators (to decrease SVRI).

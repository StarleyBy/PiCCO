Contractility: Cardiac Function Index (CFI) and Oxygen Delivery Index (DO2I)

Cardiac contractility is the ability of the myocardium to generate force during contraction. PiCCO provides several parameters for assessing contractility, including the Cardiac Function Index (CFI) and indirectly through oxygen delivery (DO2) assessment.

Cardiac Function Index (CFI)

CFI is a PiCCO parameter that provides a quantitative, volumetric assessment of the heart's global systolic function. It represents the ratio of cardiac output to global end-diastolic volume.

Calculation and Interpretation

CFI is calculated as:

CFI = CI / GEDVI

Where CI is the cardiac index and GEDVI is the Global End-Diastolic Volume Index.

Normal Values

Parameter Unit of Measurement Normal Range (Adults) Clinical Interpretation
Cardiac Function Index (CFI) L/min/m¬≤ / mL/m¬≤ 4.5 - 6.5 Optimal contractility. Below 4.5 indicates reduced contractility

Clinical Significance

  • Contractility Assessment: CFI is an objective, load-independent indicator of global myocardial contractility. Unlike CO, which is heavily dependent on preload, afterload, and heart rate, CFI attempts to normalize flow (SV) relative to available volume (GEDV)
  • Guiding Inotropic Therapy: CFI is a direct target for optimizing inotropic support. If a patient has normal preload (GEDVI) and low CI, but also has a low CFI, the primary issue is pump failure (poor contractility), indicating a need for inotropic agents (e.g., dobutamine)

Oxygen Delivery (DO2) and Oxygen Delivery Index (DO2I)

DO2I is a critical parameter that indirectly reflects the effectiveness of contractility in the context of the body's metabolic demands.

Definition and Calculation

DO2I is the indexed oxygen delivery, calculated as:

DO2I = (CI √ó CaO2) √ó 10

Where CI is the cardiac index, and CaO2 is the arterial oxygen content (in ml/dl).

Clinical Significance

DO2I is the primary goal of hemodynamic therapy, as it represents the total amount of oxygen delivered to tissues per minute. A low DO2I indicates inadequate oxygen delivery to meet metabolic demands.

DO2I Range (ml/min/m¬≤) Clinical Interpretation Clinical Consequence
High (>700) Hyperdynamic state, often unnecessary excess supply Usually seen in early sepsis or severe liver disease
Optimal/Normal 450 - 600 Adequate systemic oxygen supply
Critically Low (<450) Hypoxia/Ischemia Risk: Supply dependency Indication for immediate intervention to increase CI and/or Hgb

Relationship to Contractility

Since CI is the primary component of DO2I, parameters that improve contractility (and thus CI) also increase DO2I. Therefore, optimizing CFI and CI directly impacts the heart's ability to meet the body's metabolic demands.

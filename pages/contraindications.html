Contraindications for PiCCO Monitoring

PiCCO method application is limited by conditions that violate the fundamental assumptions of transpulmonary thermodilution (TPTD) or pulse contour analysis (PCA).

Absolute Contraindications

These conditions fundamentally make TPTD measurement invalid, making the derived cardiac output (CO), volume (GEDV), and lung water (EVLW) values unreliable, regardless of calibration:

  • Significant intracardiac shunts (left-to-right or right-to-left): Shunts drastically alter the path and mixing of the cold indicator.
    • Effect: The calculated CO_TPTD will be unreliable, and the thermodilution curve will be severely distorted, invalidating volume measurements
  • Severe aortic and/or mitral regurgitation: Severe valvular insufficiency causes significant backflow of blood, leading to recirculation of the thermal indicator in the ventricles and proximal large vessels.
    • Effect: The TPTD curve will have prolonged downslope and recirculation artifacts, leading to falsely high GEDV and ITBV values
  • Use of assist devices: CPB (cardiopulmonary bypass), ECMO / VAD (ventricular assist device): These systems create large extracorporeal shunts and circuits that completely bypass the cardiopulmonary circulation and violate the closed-loop assumptions for TPTD.
    • Effect: PiCCO is invalid when the entire circulation does not pass through the indicator

Relative Contraindications

These conditions increase technical risk or limit the utility of specific parameters:

  • Vascular injury or disease at insertion site: Severe peripheral arterial occlusive disease, local infection, or recent vascular surgery (e.g., iliac or femoral surgery) contraindicate safe insertion of the arterial catheter
  • Severe arrhythmias (e.g., atrial fibrillation, frequent PVCs): PCA relies on a relatively consistent beat-to-beat output.
    • Effect: Although PCCO can still be monitored as an average, the dynamic indices (SVV and PPV) become unreliable and should not be used for fluid management
  • Aortic counterpulsation (IABP): The mechanical action of the IABP balloon severely distorts the arterial pressure waveform.
    • Effect: PCA becomes completely invalid. TPTD can still be performed for intermittent calibration, but continuous PCCO cannot be trusted
  • Open chest cavity (open-heart surgery): Violates the intrathoracic volume assumptions.
    • Effect: GEDV and EVLWI readings are highly questionable and often unreliable

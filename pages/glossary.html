PiCCO Glossary of Terms

This section provides a glossary of key terms and abbreviations used with the PiCCO system. This will help you quickly navigate specific terminology and better understand hemodynamic parameters.

CO (Cardiac Output)
The volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute (L/min).
CI (Cardiac Index)
Cardiac output, adjusted for body surface area (L/min/m¬≤).
GEDV (Global End-Diastolic Volume)
The total volume of blood in all four heart chambers at the end of diastole (mL).
GEDVI (Global End-Diastolic Volume Index)
GEDV, adjusted for body surface area (mL/m¬≤).
EVLW (Extravascular Lung Water)
The volume of water in the extravascular space of the lungs (mL).
EVLWI (Extravascular Lung Water Index)
EVLW, adjusted for body weight (mL/kg).
SV (Stroke Volume)
The volume of blood ejected by the heart per beat (mL).
SVI (Stroke Volume Index)
SV, adjusted for body surface area (mL/m¬≤).
PVPI (Pulmonary Vascular Permeability Index)
The ratio of EVLW to pulmonary blood volume, an indicator of capillary permeability.
CVP (Central Venous Pressure)
Pressure in the vena cava and right atrium (mmHg).
MAP (Mean Arterial Pressure)
The average pressure in the arteries during one cardiac cycle (mmHg).
SVV (Stroke Volume Variation)
The percentage change in stroke volume during the respiratory cycle, a dynamic preload indicator.
PPV (Pulse Pressure Variation)
The percentage change in pulse pressure during the respiratory cycle, a dynamic preload indicator.

Troubleshooting PiCCO Issues

Various technical problems and data inaccuracies can occur during PiCCO monitoring. This section will help you quickly diagnose and fix the most common issues.

Common Problems and Solutions

1. Unstable or Inaccurate Parameter Readings

  • Possible Cause: Incorrect catheter positioning, catheter kinking, or presence of thrombus.
  • Solution: Verify catheter position with X-ray, flush the catheter, or replace if necessary.
  • Possible Cause: Issues with pressure or temperature sensors.
  • Solution: Check sensor connections, calibrate sensors, or replace if necessary.
  • Possible Cause: Improper thermodilution technique during calibration.
  • Solution: Repeat calibration, strictly following the protocol (rapid injection of cold indicator, correct volume).

2. "No Thermodynamic Signal" Error

  • Possible Cause: Issues with the temperature sensor of the PiCCO catheter.
  • Solution: Check PiCCO catheter connections, verify proper functioning of the temperature sensor. The catheter may be damaged.
  • Possible Cause: Insufficient temperature difference between indicator and blood.
  • Solution: Use a colder indicator (chilled saline from refrigerator) or increase the volume of indicator.

3. High Extravascular Lung Water Index (EVLWI) Without Clinical Signs of Edema

  • Possible Cause: Incorrect indicator volume or temperature during calibration.
  • Solution: Recalibrate the system, carefully controlling indicator volume and temperature.
  • Possible Cause: Severe respiratory failure or significant pulmonary edema that is not always obvious on superficial examination.
  • Solution: Correlate readings with other clinical data and instrumental examinations (chest X-ray, lung ultrasound).

For problems not described here, consult the PiCCO user manual or contact the manufacturer's technical support.

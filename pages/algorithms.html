Clinical Decision Algorithms Using PiCCO

PiCCO-based algorithms are physiology- driven decision frameworks, not rigid protocols. Their purpose is to identify which component of oxygen transport is failing and to apply targeted therapy with minimal iatrogenic harm.

Foundational Principles (Must Be Understood Before Any Algorithm)

  • Cardiac index alone never defines adequacy of perfusion.
  • Preload status ‚â† fluid responsiveness.
  • Dynamic indices are valid only under strict conditions.
  • Extravascular lung water is a therapeutic constraint, not just a diagnostic marker.
  • Every intervention must be followed by re-measurement.

  • Assess signs of tissue hypoperfusion (lactate, capillary refill, urine output).
  • Evaluate EVLWI and PVPI before considering fluids.

Step 4. Afterload & Arterial Tone

Hypotension is not synonymous with vasodilation. SVRI must be interpreted in the context of CI and MAP.

Pattern Interpretation Therapy
Low MAP + low SVRI Pathological vasodilation Vasopressors
Low CI + high SVRI Afterload mismatch Reduce resistance if perfusion allows

Step 5. Contractility Evaluation

With adequate preload and appropriate afterload, persistent low CI reflects myocardial dysfunction.

  • CFI and GEF assess global systolic performance.
  • Low values indicate need for inotropic support.
  • Always reassess EVLWI after inotrope initiation.

Step 6. Iterative Reassessment

PiCCO-guided optimization is a continuous loop. Each intervention mandates re-evaluation of: CI, GEDVI, EVLWI, SVRI, lactate and clinical status.

Algorithm 3 ‚Äî Fluid Intolerance & De- resuscitation

High EVLWI with declining oxygenation or rising driving pressures indicates the need to remove fluid, even if CI is low.

  • Diuretics or RRT should be guided by EVLWI trends.
  • CFI-guided inotropes may permit safe fluid removal.

Algorithm 4 ‚Äî Septic Myocardial Dysfunction

  • Often masked by normal/high CI.
  • Low CFI despite adequate preload suggests myocardial depression.
  • Early inotropes may reduce vasopressor dose and EVLWI.

PiCCO does not replace clinical judgment. It makes physiology visible.

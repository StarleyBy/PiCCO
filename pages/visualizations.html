PiCCO Hemodynamic Visualizations and Diagrams

This section presents key visual materials illustrating the physiological concepts, parameters, and algorithms used in PiCCO monitoring.

Key Schemes and Diagrams

Since this site is static, visual materials are presented as schematic descriptions that can be used to find corresponding illustrations or for better understanding of concepts.

1. Frank-Starling Curve

Purpose: Illustration of the fundamental relationship between preload stretch of cardiac muscle and contractile force.

  • Components:
    • X-Axis (Horizontal): Preload (End-diastolic volume or GEDVI)
    • Y-Axis (Vertical): Stroke Volume (SV) or Cardiac Index (CI)
    • Ascending limb: Region where the heart is preload-dependent. Volume increase leads to significant SV increase.
    • Plateau: Region where the heart is preload-independent. Further volume increase does not lead to significant SV increase.
  • Clinical significance: SVV and PPV help determine if the patient is on the ascending segment (fluid responsive) or plateau (non-responsive).

2. DO2/VO2 Curve (Oxygen Delivery/Consumption)

Purpose: Shows the balance between oxygen delivery (DO2) and consumption (VO2) in tissues.

  • Components:
    • X-Axis (Horizontal): Oxygen delivery (DO2)
    • Y-Axis (Vertical): Oxygen consumption (VO2)
    • Supply-independent zone: Region where VO2 remains constant despite moderate changes in DO2. Tissues use only what they need.
    • Critical point: Point where DO2 is too low to sustain VO2 demand. Oxygen extraction reserve is exhausted.
    • Supply-dependent zone: Region where any further reduction in DO2 causes direct VO2 reduction, leading to anaerobic metabolism.
  • Clinical significance: In shock, patients are supply-dependent, leading to lactate elevation.

3. PiCCO Shock Matrix (CI vs. SVRI)

Purpose: Simplified two-dimensional matrix for classifying shock types by primary hemodynamic parameters.

  • Components:
    • Y-Axis (Vertical): Cardiac Index (CI)
    • X-Axis (Horizontal): Systemic Vascular Resistance Index (SVRI)
    • Upper-left quadrant: CI ‚Üë and SVRI ‚Üì ‚Üí Distributive shock (e.g., sepsis)
    • Lower-left quadrant: CI ‚Üì and SVRI ‚Üì ‚Üí Absolute hypovolemia or Anaphylactic/Neurogenic shock
    • Lower-right quadrant: CI ‚Üì and SVRI ‚Üë ‚Üí Cardiogenic shock or Delayed hypovolemic
    • Upper-right quadrant: CI ‚Üë and SVRI ‚Üë ‚Üí Compensatory/Over-resuscitation
  • Clinical significance: Helps quickly identify shock type and determine the primary therapeutic focus.

4. Pulmonary Edema Diagnostic Matrix (EVLWI vs. PVPI)

Purpose: Determining the cause of pulmonary edema by two key PiCCO parameters.

  • Components:
    • Y-Axis (Vertical): Extravascular Lung Water Index (EVLWI)
    • X-Axis (Horizontal): Pulmonary Vascular Permeability Index (PVPI)
    • High EVLWI + Normal PVPI: Hydrostatic edema (e.g., cardiogenic) ‚Üí Treatment: Diuretics/Fluid removal
    • High EVLWI + High PVPI: Permeability edema (e.g., ARDS/Sepsis) ‚Üí Treatment: Fluid restriction
    • Normal EVLWI: No significant pulmonary edema
  • Clinical significance: Determines fluid management strategy in presence of pulmonary dysfunction.

5. PiCCO Monitoring Scheme (Catheter Placement)

Purpose: Shows proper anatomical placement and connection of PiCCO system components.

  • Elements:
    • Central Venous Catheter (CVC): Tip in right atrium (RA) or superior vena cava (SVC) ‚Üí for cold indicator injection
    • PiCCO Arterial Catheter: Specialized catheter with thermistor at tip placed in descending aorta (usually via femoral artery) ‚Üí for temperature signal detection
    • Pressure transducer: Connected to arterial line ‚Üí for arterial pressure measurement and pulse contour analysis
    • Injection temperature sensor: On CVC injection line ‚Üí for injection temperature measurement
  • Clinical significance: Proper placement is critical for measurement accuracy.

PiCCO Measured Parameters

The PiCCO system provides a wide range of hemodynamic parameters to assess the patient's cardiovascular status and fluid balance. Understanding these parameters is critical for clinical decision-making.

Key Parameters and their Interpretation

  • Cardiac Output (CO/CI): The volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute. The index (CI) accounts for body surface area. Normal values: CO 4-8 L/min, CI 2.5-4 L/min/m¬≤.
  • Global End-Diastolic Volume (GEDV/GEDI): An indicator of left and right ventricular preload. Reflects the total blood volume in the heart chambers at the end of diastole. Normal: GEDI 680-800 mL/m¬≤.
  • Extravascular Lung Water (EVLW/EVLWI): The amount of water in the interstitial space of the lungs. An important indicator for assessing pulmonary edema. Normal: EVLWI up to 7 mL/kg.
  • Pulmonary Vascular Permeability Index (PVPI): Reflects the permeability of lung capillaries. Used in combination with EVLWI to differentiate the causes of pulmonary edema (cardiogenic or non-cardiogenic). Normal: PVPI 1.0-3.0.
  • Stroke Volume (SV/SVI): The volume of blood ejected by the heart per beat. Normal: SVI 35-60 mL/m¬≤.
  • Stroke Volume Variation (SVV) and Pulse Pressure Variation (PPV): Dynamic parameters used to assess fluid responsiveness. Normal: SVV/PPV less than 10-12%.

Importance of Comprehensive Assessment

It is necessary to evaluate all parameters comprehensively, taking into account the patient's clinical picture, concomitant diseases, and ongoing therapy. Isolated assessment of a single parameter can lead to incorrect conclusions.

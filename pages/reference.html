PiCCO Parameters Reference Guide

This section compiles all the main parameters used in the PiCCO system, with explanations of their meaning, normal values, and clinical applications.

Glossary of Main Terms

Term / Abbreviation Definition
Afterload Resistance that the ventricle must overcome to eject blood into the systemic circulation (assessed by SVRI).
ARDS Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome. Severe type of permeability pulmonary edema (PVPI ‚Üë) with refractory hypoxemia.
BSA Body Surface Area. Used for indexing many hemodynamic parameters.
C(a-v)O2 Arterio-venous oxygen difference. Difference between arterial and venous oxygen content. Key component of the Fick principle.
CABG Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting. Surgical procedure to restore blood flow to the myocardium.
Ca2+ Calcium ions. Key second messenger regulating myocardial contractility (inotropy).
CaO2 Arterial oxygen content. Total amount of oxygen carried in arterial blood (mainly Hb-bound).

CO Cardiac Output. Volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute (SV √ó HR).
Contractility Intrinsic ability of the myocardium to develop force and shorten, independent of changes in preload and afterload (inotropy).
CVP Central Venous Pressure. Pressure in the superior vena cava or right atrium. Poor surrogate for preload.
CvO2 Central venous oxygen content. Total amount of oxygen carried in mixed venous blood.
ŒîT Temperature difference. Difference between blood and injectate solution temperature; important for thermal dilution accuracy.

Fick Principle Fick Principle. Principle used to calculate VO2 and CO based on arterio-venous oxygen content difference.
Fluid Responsive State in which increasing preload (fluid administration) leads to significant increase in cardiac output (working on the ascending portion of the Frank-Starling curve).
Frank-Starling Law Frank-Starling Law. Fundamental physiological principle stating that SV increases with EDV, all other factors being equal.
GEDV Global End-diastolic Volume. Absolute volume of blood in all four chambers of the heart at end-diastole.
GEDVI Global End-diastolic Volume Index. GEDV, indexed to BSA. Primary static preload indicator for PiCCO. Target: 680 - 800 mL/m¬≤.
Hemoglobin (Hb) Protein in red blood cells responsible for binding and transporting most of the oxygen in the blood.
Hyperdynamic State State (e.g., severe sepsis) characterized by high CI and often low SVRI (vasodilation).
Inotropy Term for the intrinsic contractile state of the myocardium (synonymous with contractility).
ITBV Intrathoracic Blood Volume. Total volume of blood in the thoracic cavity (heart and pulmonary vessels).
ITBVI Intrathoracic Blood Volume Index. ITBV, indexed to BSA. Another preload indicator for PiCCO.
L-type Ca2+ channels Voltage-sensitive calcium channels in cardiomyocytes important for initiating contraction.
Laplace's Law Laplace's Law. Principle relating wall tension in the ventricle to internal pressure and chamber radius (key determinant of afterload).
LV Failure Left Ventricular Failure; decreased ability of the LV to eject blood, often leading to hydrostatic pulmonary edema.
MAP Mean Arterial Pressure. Average pressure in the arterial system over a cardiac cycle.
MTt Mean Transit time. Mean time it takes for the cold indicator to travel from injection to detection. Used for calculating volumetric parameters.
Myocardial Stunning Temporary post-ischemic mechanical dysfunction of the myocardium, recovering over hours or days, often after CABG.
O2ER Oxygen Extraction Ratio. Fraction of delivered oxygen (DO2) consumed by tissues (VO2).
PAOP Pulmonary Artery Occlusion Pressure. Pressure measured in the blocked pulmonary artery; historical surrogate for LV preload.
PEEP Positive End-Expiratory Pressure. Maintained pressure in the lungs at end-expiration. Affects RV preload and EVLWI measurement.
Permeability Edema Pulmonary edema caused by damage to the capillary-alveolar barrier (PVPI ‚Üë), causing leak (e.g., ARDS).
PiCCO Pulse Contour Cardiac Output. Technology using transpulmonary thermodilution (TPTD) and pulse contour analysis (PCA) for extended hemodynamic monitoring.
Preload Mechanical state of the heart at end-diastole, representing the degree of stretch of myocardial fibers before contraction (assessed by GEDVI).
PPV Pulse Pressure Variation. Dynamic parameter reflecting changes in arterial pulse pressure over the respiratory cycle (analog to SVV).
PVPI Pulmonary Vascular Permeability Index. Reflects integrity of alveolar-capillary barrier. High PVPI (>1.7) indicates permeability edema.
Right Atrium (RA) Chamber where the tip of the central venous catheter is often placed for bolus injection.
RR Respiratory Rate. Number of breaths per minute. Important for interpreting SVV/PPV.
RV Failure Right Ventricular Failure; decreased ability of the RV to pump blood into the pulmonary circulation.
Septic Cardiomyopathy Reversible myocardial dysfunction associated with severe sepsis, characterized by low CFI despite high CI.
Shock State of cellular and tissue hypoxia due to mismatch between oxygen delivery (DO2) and consumption (VO2).
Stroke Volume SV Volume of blood ejected by the ventricle in a single contraction.

SvO2 Mixed Venous Saturation. Oxygen saturation of blood returning to the right atrium. Clinical indicator of DO2/VO2 balance.
TBSA Total Body Surface Area. Used in burn protocols and for parameter indexing.
Thermistor Temperature sensor located at the tip of the PiCCO arterial catheter.
